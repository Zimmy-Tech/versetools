Real DPS — Server Tick Quantization

Some weapons use sequence firing (multi-barrel repeaters), where each shot is scheduled on the server's 30 Hz tick clock. This quantizes the fire rate to discrete steps:

effective RPM = 1800 ÷ ceil(1800 ÷ listed RPM)

If the listed RPM doesn't land exactly on a tick boundary, the weapon fires slower than advertised. Real DPS reflects this actual in-game fire rate.

Example: The Killshot Rifle lists 535 RPM, but ceil(1800/535) = 4 ticks per shot, giving an effective 450 RPM — a 15.9% DPS loss.

Weapons showing — in this column are unaffected (single-shot cannons, gatlings, or weapons that already sit on a tick boundary).

Recoil — Pitch, Yaw & Smooth

Three values sourced from each weapon's procedural recoil curve:

Pitch (°) — maximum vertical kick per shot, in degrees. This is how far the camera deflects upward when the weapon fires. Higher = harder to keep on-target in sustained fire.

Yaw (°) — maximum horizontal kick per shot, in degrees. Adds side-to-side deflection on top of the vertical kick.

Smooth (s) — recoil recovery time, in seconds. How long the camera takes to settle back after a shot. Lower = snappier return, higher = softer / smoother recoil feel.

Example: An A03 Sniper kicks ~0.88° up and ~0.2° sideways per shot, settling in 0.09s — a sharp, high-impact single-shot. A P4-AR Rifle kicks only ~0.19° up at 0.06s smooth — flat and controllable for full-auto.

Weapons showing — have no recoil curve (launchers, railgun) or all-zero values in game files (most energy sidearms).
