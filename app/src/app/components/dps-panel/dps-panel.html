What is Real DPS?

This data is based on real in-game testing and results. It is my best understanding of how the system works, calculated to an error rate of less than 1.5%. It could possibly be incorrect, however, enough data points were checked that it seems incredibly likely. The rationale is listed here as well as in the formulas tab.

The fire rates listed in the game data for many weapons are not what you actually get in-game. We tested this by timing ammo consumption across 9 different weapons and found a consistent pattern.

The Problem

The game server runs on a clock that ticks 30 times per second. Every tick, the server checks: "is it time to fire another shot?" If yes, fire. If no, wait for the next tick.

A weapon that wants to fire every 80ms (750 RPM) gets delayed:

  • Tick 1 (0ms): FIRE
  • Tick 2 (33ms): not yet
  • Tick 3 (66ms): not yet
  • Tick 4 (100ms): 100ms > 80ms → FIRE

Instead of 80ms between shots, you get 100ms. That's 600 RPM instead of 750.

Which Weapons Are Affected?

Repeaters (multi-barrel cycling weapons) are affected. Gatlings and cannons use a different firing mechanism and are not affected — their listed rates are accurate.

The Formula

Real RPM = 1800 / ceil(1800 / Listed RPM)
